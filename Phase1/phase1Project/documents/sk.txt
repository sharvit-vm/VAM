Insurance Fundamentals
Aggregator: Who compares different policies 
Distributor: who provides their own policies
1. Insurance: Risk transfer mechanism, Policy holder(insured)-> insurer (insurance company)

2. Pooling concept: Bringing similar type of risks together, ex: An auto insurance company provides auto insurances of all types, and they might have sub pools ex: Maharashtra, Telangana etc. or age groups. 

3. Law of large numbers: 
When a large number of similar risks are grouped together, the average outcome becomes more predictable.
 


4. Risk: Possibility of loss 
	4.1. Pure risk: Something which is definitely going to damage if it has to happen. 
	4.2 speculative risk: ex: mutual funds, which can give us gains or loss. 

5. Peril: reason behind the loss, ex: damage to house due to fire -> peril: fire. 
	5.1 Open perils: Most of the coverages will be covered except exclusions. 
	5.2 Named Perils: Only for specific perils the loss will be covered.
